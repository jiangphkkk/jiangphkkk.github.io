Careers & growth

PEOPLE

Grow under real launch pressure

You will trade off scope, run release checklists, and post-mortem incidents—learning the gap between “it runs” and “it survives traffic and store review,” and shaping your own quality bar.

Write it well, explain it clearly

We capture decisions with decks, notes, and screen recordings. Good communication cuts rework and helps new teammates align fast.

Depth in tech, sense for the business

Whether you lean toward front-end, client, or data, you will touch funnels, push, and monetization—so technical choices map to measurable outcomes, not isolated cleverness.

Regular internal talks

Market teardowns, competitor sketches, and toolchain cheat sheets rotate on a biweekly forum; strong write-ups go into the internal library for others to reuse.

Cross-functional checks before big releases

Product, design, engineering, and growth review major versions together—copy, assets, and instrumentation stay aligned so nothing ships with mismatched messaging.
